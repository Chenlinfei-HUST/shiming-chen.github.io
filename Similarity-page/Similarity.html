Similarity-DT: Kernel similarity embedding for Dynamic Texture Synthesis

Shiming Chen, Peng Zhang, and Xinge You

Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST), China


Abstract

Dynamic texture exhibits statistical stationary in spatial domian and stochastic repetitiveness in temporal dimension, which is key cues for distinguishing dynamic texture videos from other videos. In this study, a simple yet effective dynamic texture synthesis method (Similarity-DT) is proposed. Different from existing methods where a proper dimensionality-reduction function is used for conveniently solving transition function to learn the representation of dynamic textures or deep neural networks are applied to learn a dynamic texture synthesis model, our method integrates kernel learning and extreme learning machine (ELM) into a powfully unified synthesis system to learn kernel similarity embedding for representing dynamic texture sequences. Specifically, the method uses kernel function to replace feature mapping function of hidden layer of extreme learning machine, and thus the kernel similarity embedding is easily learned after training and high-fidelity dynamic texture sequences can be synthesized iteratively by model. The experimental results on well-known benchmark datasets showed that our proposed dynamic texture synthesis method can effectively generate realistic dynamic textures with fast speed. We also showed that our method is capable of preserving long-term temporal continuity of the synthesized dynamic texture sequences and achieves state-of-the-art performance.

Evaluation

There are two different benchmark datasets used for evaluating our method: Gatech Graphcut Textures dataset and Dyntex dataset. Some of these generated dynamic texture videos are used in paper, in which supports better vision quality. Indeed, we also evaluate our method on others DTs in-the-wild. If you are interested in the quantitative evaluation results (PSNR, SSIM), please take it from our paper.
