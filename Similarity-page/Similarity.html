Similarity-DT: Kernel similarity embedding for Dynamic Texture Synthesis

Shiming Chen1, Xinge You1, Peng Zhang1, Qinmu Peng1, and Zehong Cao2

1Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST), China

2University of Technology Sydney (UTS), Australia


Abstract

Dynamic texture exhibits statistical stationary in spatial domian and stochastic repetitiveness in temporal dimension, which is key cues for distinguishing dynamic texture videos from other videos. In this study, a simple yet effective dynamic texture synthesis method (Similarity-DT) is proposed. Different from existing methods where a proper dimensionality-reduction function is used for conveniently solving transition function to learn the representation of dynamic textures or deep neural networks are applied to learn a dynamic texture synthesis model, our method integrates kernel learning and extreme learning machine (ELM) into a powful unified synthesis system to learn kernel similarity embedding, which elegantly represents spatial-temporal transition of frame-to-frame of dynamic textures. Specifically, our method uses kernel function to replace feature mapping function of hidden layer of extreme learning machine, and thus the kernel similarity embedding is easily learned after training and high-fidelity dynamic texture sequences can be synthesized iteratively by model. The experimental results on well-known benchmark datasets showed that our method can effectively generate realistic dynamic textures with fast speed. We also showed that our method is capable of preserving long-term temporal continuity of the synthesized dynamic texture sequences and achieves state-of-the-art performance.
